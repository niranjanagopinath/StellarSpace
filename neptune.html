F A C T S

Neptune was discovered in 1846 by Urbain Le Verrier, John Couch Adams, and Johann Galle. Only Voyager 2 has visited Neptune.
It's an ice giant. It is made of a thick soup of water, ammonia, and methane flowing over a solid core about the size of Earth.
Its atmosphere is made of hydrogen, helium, and methane. The methane gives Neptune the blue color. Neptune has six rings, but they're very hard to see.
